## Expected metric, risks, and rollback

- Expected immediate metric: professor-pilot completion quality, not a product lift. The protocol should produce intake completion, package generation, usefulness, actual time, corrections, accepted-revision, second-run repeatability, preferred purchase format, and payment-oriented commitment evidence.
- Target after five comparable pilots: at least 4/5 uncoached intake/package completions, 4/5 usefulness at least 4/5 after the second run, 3/5 useful accepted corrections, 3/5 preference for ongoing revision support, and 2/5 payment-oriented commitments, with no unresolved critical privacy/academic-control defect.
- Product risk: the generic engine may be too broad for assignment redesign; the pilot is designed to expose this before a specialized template is built.
- Commercial risk: university-provided AI/support and one-time resources may defeat the individual subscription thesis.
- Research risk: a single pilot cannot validate the market; five comparable pilots are the precommitted decision sample.
- CI risk: GitHub Actions is unverified until the first remote run.
- Rollback for today's product files: revert the first Git commit or remove the three verification scripts, CI workflow, package scripts/dependency, validation artifact, and documentation entries. No stored-user-data or runtime-service migration exists.
