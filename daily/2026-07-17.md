## Highest-priority obstacle

The product had no way to convert a failed real run into an approved correction. That absence blocked the core trust promise and the intended recurring subscription value: the user could download a first package, but could not report an unmet need, review a correction, or control whether it replaced working behavior. Current one-time redesign competitors and free university programs make this loop more strategically important than adding another static workflow artifact.

## Work completed

- Added validated feedback capture for usefulness (1–5), failed step, failure category, report, desired outcome, actual time, and correction count.
- Added a deterministic local correction draft for one reported step. Category-specific strategies address accuracy, clarity, missing inputs, tool fit, time, or another unmet need.
- The draft adds an action correction, accountable human review, and a safe fallback while leaving every unrelated step unchanged.
- Added a side-by-side current-versus-proposed review. The current package remains active and downloadable until the user explicitly chooses “Approve and replace package.”
- “Keep current package” discards the draft without changing active behavior.
- Added a clear disclosure that the correction is a local rule-based prototype of the future AI-assisted flow. No external AI, server, database, or durable feedback storage was introduced.
- Added four focused regression tests for validation, isolated change scope, preserved review/fallback, immutability, and approval gating.

## Expected metric, risks, and rollback

- Expected future metrics: complaint-to-draft completion, draft approval rate, complaints converted into accepted revisions, usefulness after the next run, and repeatability. No analytics exist, so no lift is claimed.
- Validation target: one professor completes a real assignment-redesign or lecture workflow, rates usefulness, reports one genuine miss, decides whether the correction resolves it, and runs the approved revision again.
- Product risk: transparent rules can test the approval UX but cannot produce the contextual correction quality expected from a production AI/human review service.
- Commercial risk: one-time paid workbooks, low-cost courses, free cohorts, and institution-subsidized support may cap individual subscription WTP.
- Privacy risk is unchanged because feedback is held only in component memory for the current page session and is not sent or persisted.
- Rollback: remove `src/revision.ts`/`src/revision.test.ts`, the `CorrectionLoop` render/component/imports, correction styles, and the documentation additions. No schema, backend, stored-data format, or external service changed.
