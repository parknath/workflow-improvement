## Current professor, buyer, competitor, privacy, and market evidence

### Need varies by assignment and discipline

- Berkeley’s study of 31,000 syllabi found faculty moving from blanket AI rules to task-specific policies, with large disciplinary differences; business courses adopted new AI tasks faster while humanities remained more restrictive. This makes normalized discipline and assignment-type evidence necessary before generalizing a pilot result: https://cshe.berkeley.edu/news/new-study-31000-college-syllabi-shows-faculty-warming-ai-classroom
- A professor discussion posted July 22 describes composition instructors breaking one complex paper into weekly evidence, invention, outline, consultation, and reflection stages while managing accommodation and workload constraints. It is a current qualitative need signal, not product-specific demand proof: https://www.reddit.com/r/Professors/comments/1v3hc3h/composition_professors_what_are_you_doing_about_ai/
- EDUCAUSE argues that listening to faculty concerns is a precondition for precise, trustworthy responses that respect faculty time. This supports uncoached observation and disconfirming evidence rather than feature-led assumptions: https://er.educause.edu/articles/2026/4/listening-to-skepticism-what-faculty-concerns-about-generative-ai-reveal

### Privacy and institutional approval are operational constraints

- The University of Florida’s 2026 guidance requires approved tools for sensitive/restricted data, critical evaluation, human involvement in important decisions, and course-specific AI expectations. A generic privacy statement cannot substitute for the participant’s real institutional policy: https://ai.ufl.edu/guidelines/
- Stanford launched institution-funded ChatGPT Edu, Gemini Enterprise, and Claude for Education access in June, citing better protection and pricing than individual purchases. - OpenAI’s current ChatGPT Edu documentation emphasizes institution-level security, permissions, SSO/SCIM, and no training on business data. Institution-provided general AI is therefore both a substitute and a trust benchmark: https://help.openai.com/en/articles/9377311-chatgpt-edu-at-openai

- Sensei now promises a complete aligned course draft with Canvas-ready structure in about 15 minutes and no credit card. ## Highest-priority obstacle

Workflow Lab still lacks a real, uncoached, comparable two-session professor pilot and payment-oriented commitment. Within that blocker, the machine-readable evidence gate could not reliably show whether results were concentrated by discipline, course level, assignment type, recurrence, policy status, substitute, or actual funding access. Applying a broad keep/change decision to such records would be unreliable.

## Change threshold and justification

- **Verified problem:** the precommitted protocol explicitly allows narrowing by discipline, course type, policy condition, recurring need, substitute, and payer, but schema version 1.0 retained discipline/course level as unnormalized text and omitted structured assignment type, workflow frequency, policy status, actual substitute/spend, and available funding path.
- **Why highest leverage:** the next five pilots are the critical path. If their context cannot be compared, the result may create false confidence or force interviews to be repeated.
- **Expected outcome and metric:** 100% of records counted as complete must contain normalized decision context, current substitute and personal spend, and a checked funding path. Cohort output must show coverage for six decision dimensions and warn when a passing cohort is concentrated.
- **Why preferable to no change:** no customer-facing change remains correct, but leaving the evidence defect would undermine the only justified next experiment.
- **Strongest practical alternative:** build accounts, billing, analytics, another workflow, or AI revision. Those actions are approval-dependent or speculative and do not resolve buyer/retention uncertainty. A recruitment message alone would help scheduling but would not make resulting evidence decision-safe.
- **Validation needed:** schema-valid template/example, placeholder rejection, completeness checks for substitute/spend/funding, synthetic exclusion, coverage counts, concentration warnings, unchanged precommitted outcome gates, strict type checking, full product verification, Pages build, remote CI, deployment health, and rollback.

## Risks, blockers, and rollback

- Better evidence structure does not recruit or schedule a professor. The next result still requires owner-led or approved outreach and two real sessions.
- Five pilots remain directional. Coverage warnings reveal concentration but cannot make a small sample representative.
- Funding availability is not willingness to pay, and reimbursement is not personal-card demand. The evaluator keeps these separate but cannot prevent biased interviewing.
- Completed records may contain qualitative notes. Keep them coded, private, access-controlled, uncommitted, and free of student or restricted data.
- Sellable-beta accounts, subscription billing, durable cross-device storage, approved product analytics, and multi-user authorization remain incomplete and approval-dependent.
- Roll back the implementation with `git revert 32d6005` and push `main`; the deployment workflows republish the prior immutable application state.
