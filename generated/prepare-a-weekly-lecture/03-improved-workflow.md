# Prepare a weekly lecture

Create a clear 90-minute lecture for first-year students

Version 0.1

## 1. Create a standardized session workspace

- Purpose: Produce named session workspace reliably.
- Input: Course code, week, and session topic
- Action: Create the session folder from the standard structure and apply the naming rule.
- Tool: Google Drive
- Output: Named session workspace
- AI involvement: None; this is a rule-based setup step.
- Human review: Confirm the course, week, and access permissions.
- Time target: 5 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if the session identity or approved storage location is unclear.

## 2. Collect source materials

- Purpose: Produce source manifest reliably.
- Input: Syllabus, previous lecture, textbook chapter, and approved references
- Action: Copy or link the required sources into the session workspace and record gaps.
- Tool: Google Drive
- Output: Source manifest
- AI involvement: AI may summarize the manifest only after the professor supplies approved sources.
- Human review: Confirm every source is relevant and permitted for use.
- Time target: 10 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if a required source is missing or its provenance is unknown.

## 3. Define learning objectives

- Purpose: Produce approved learning objectives reliably.
- Input: Syllabus outcomes and previous-session context
- Action: Complete the learning-objective template with observable outcomes and scope boundaries.
- Tool: Documents
- Output: Approved learning objectives
- AI involvement: AI may propose wording; it may not decide the teaching scope.
- Human review: The professor approves objectives and alignment with the course.
- Time target: 10 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if objectives cannot be tied to the syllabus.

## 4. Generate the lecture brief

- Purpose: Produce lecture brief reliably.
- Input: Approved objectives and source manifest
- Action: Run the lecture-brief instruction to draft prerequisites, concepts, likely misconceptions, and evidence to verify.
- Tool: Approved AI tool and Documents
- Output: Lecture brief
- AI involvement: AI creates a source-grounded first draft and flags uncertainty.
- Human review: Verify every academic claim and remove material outside the course level.
- Time target: 15 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if the draft introduces unsupported claims.

## 5. Create the lesson structure

- Purpose: Produce timed lesson structure reliably.
- Input: Approved objectives and lecture brief
- Action: Allocate time to opening, explanation, examples, practice, discussion, and close.
- Tool: Documents
- Output: Timed lesson structure
- AI involvement: AI may suggest an initial sequence; the professor selects the pedagogy.
- Human review: Confirm pacing, emphasis, and teaching sequence.
- Time target: 12 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if the plan exceeds the session length.

## 6. Plan slides

- Purpose: Produce slide plan and speaker-note outline reliably.
- Input: Timed lesson structure and source manifest
- Action: Complete the slide-planning template with one purpose and evidence source per slide group.
- Tool: PowerPoint
- Output: Slide plan and speaker-note outline
- AI involvement: AI may draft concise headings and notes from approved material.
- Human review: Review accuracy, accessibility, and cognitive load.
- Time target: 25 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if a slide cannot be traced to an approved source or objective.

## 7. Create an activity or discussion

- Purpose: Produce class activity reliably.
- Input: Learning objectives and lesson structure
- Action: Use the activity template to define instructions, timing, expected response, and debrief.
- Tool: Documents
- Output: Class activity
- AI involvement: AI may propose variants; it must not evaluate identifiable students.
- Human review: The professor approves feasibility, inclusivity, and alignment.
- Time target: 12 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if the activity does not support an objective.

## 8. Adapt material to the student level

- Purpose: Produce level-adjusted teaching package reliably.
- Input: Slide plan, activity, and cohort context
- Action: Run the student-level checklist and revise terminology, examples, scaffolding, and assumed knowledge.
- Tool: Documents and PowerPoint
- Output: Level-adjusted teaching package
- AI involvement: AI may flag jargon or missing explanations without using student records.
- Human review: The professor decides what is appropriate for the cohort.
- Time target: 10 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if adaptation would require identifiable student information.

## 9. Review academic accuracy

- Purpose: Produce accuracy-approved draft reliably.
- Input: Complete draft and source manifest
- Action: Verify claims, citations, examples, calculations, and distinctions between fact and interpretation.
- Tool: PowerPoint and source materials
- Output: Accuracy-approved draft
- AI involvement: AI may produce a review checklist but cannot approve content.
- Human review: The professor performs and records final academic review.
- Time target: 15 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop and return to the relevant step when a claim cannot be verified.

## 10. Export the final teaching package

- Purpose: Produce final teaching package reliably.
- Input: Accuracy-approved draft
- Action: Export slides, notes, activity instructions, and source manifest using the naming convention.
- Tool: PowerPoint and Google Drive
- Output: Final teaching package
- AI involvement: None; export uses a standard checklist.
- Human review: Confirm files open correctly and only intended materials are shared.
- Time target: 5 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if permissions or exported files cannot be verified.

## 11. Complete a post-class reflection

- Purpose: Produce post-class reflection reliably.
- Input: Delivered session and teaching observations
- Action: Record what worked, timing variance, misconceptions, and one change for the next run.
- Tool: Documents
- Output: Post-class reflection
- AI involvement: AI may later summarize non-sensitive reflections across sessions.
- Human review: The professor decides which changes should carry forward.
- Time target: 5 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if reflection notes would expose identifiable student information.
