# Prepare a weekly lecture

Create a clear 90-minute lecture for first-year students

Version 0.1

## Before starting

- Confirm the workflow trigger has occurred.
- Gather the named inputs in the approved workspace.
- Confirm the desired output and definition of done.
- Name the person responsible for every human review.
- Start a timer for each step.

## Run sequence

1. **Create a standardized session workspace** — produce Named session workspace; target 5 minutes.
2. **Collect source materials** — produce Source manifest; target 10 minutes.
3. **Define learning objectives** — produce Approved learning objectives; target 10 minutes.
4. **Generate the lecture brief** — produce Lecture brief; target 15 minutes.
5. **Create the lesson structure** — produce Timed lesson structure; target 12 minutes.
6. **Plan slides** — produce Slide plan and speaker-note outline; target 25 minutes.
7. **Create an activity or discussion** — produce Class activity; target 12 minutes.
8. **Adapt material to the student level** — produce Level-adjusted teaching package; target 10 minutes.
9. **Review academic accuracy** — produce Accuracy-approved draft; target 15 minutes.
10. **Export the final teaching package** — produce Final teaching package; target 5 minutes.
11. **Complete a post-class reflection** — produce Post-class reflection; target 5 minutes.

## Human checkpoints

- **Create a standardized session workspace:** Confirm the course, week, and access permissions.
- **Collect source materials:** Confirm every source is relevant and permitted for use.
- **Define learning objectives:** The professor approves objectives and alignment with the course.
- **Generate the lecture brief:** Verify every academic claim and remove material outside the course level.
- **Create the lesson structure:** Confirm pacing, emphasis, and teaching sequence.
- **Plan slides:** Review accuracy, accessibility, and cognitive load.
- **Create an activity or discussion:** The professor approves feasibility, inclusivity, and alignment.
- **Adapt material to the student level:** The professor decides what is appropriate for the cohort.
- **Review academic accuracy:** The professor performs and records final academic review.
- **Export the final teaching package:** Confirm files open correctly and only intended materials are shared.
- **Complete a post-class reflection:** The professor decides which changes should carry forward.

## Stop and escalate

When a failure condition is met, do not improvise around missing evidence, privacy restrictions, or accountable decisions. Return to the named input owner or reviewer and record the interruption for the next workflow revision.
