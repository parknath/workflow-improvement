# Plan an academic week

Create a realistic plan for classes, assignments, study, and project work

Version 0.1

## 1. Collect classes, commitments, and deadlines

- Purpose: Produce complete weekly intake reliably.
- Input: Course pages, email, calendar, and unfinished work
- Action: Review each approved source once and record every dated commitment in the weekly intake.
- Tool: LMS, Email, and Calendar
- Output: Complete weekly intake
- AI involvement: AI is not needed for collection; source links remain visible.
- Human review: The student confirms dates against official course sources.
- Time target: 15 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if a deadline is ambiguous and verify it with the instructor or syllabus.

## 2. Identify assignments

- Purpose: Produce assignment and commitment list reliably.
- Input: Complete weekly intake
- Action: Separate assessed work, preparation, administration, and personal commitments.
- Tool: Notes
- Output: Assignment and commitment list
- AI involvement: AI may group entries but cannot invent requirements.
- Human review: Confirm every assignment against its official instructions.
- Time target: 5 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if requirements or submission dates are missing.

## 3. Break large assignments into smaller tasks

- Purpose: Produce actionable task list reliably.
- Input: Assignment instructions and rubric
- Action: Use the decomposition template to create actions that can be completed in one work block.
- Tool: Notes
- Output: Actionable task list
- AI involvement: AI may draft a decomposition from supplied instructions.
- Human review: The student verifies that every rubric requirement is covered.
- Time target: 10 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if the assignment instructions have not been provided.

## 4. Estimate effort

- Purpose: Produce ranged effort estimates reliably.
- Input: Actionable task list and prior experience
- Action: Assign a low, expected, and high estimate to each task.
- Tool: Effort worksheet
- Output: Ranged effort estimates
- AI involvement: AI may suggest a starting range; the student owns the estimate.
- Human review: Adjust estimates for personal pace and current capacity.
- Time target: 8 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if a task is still too vague to estimate.

## 5. Identify dependencies

- Purpose: Produce dependency-aware task list reliably.
- Input: Actionable task list
- Action: Mark tasks that require another task, person, resource, or fixed event first.
- Tool: Notes
- Output: Dependency-aware task list
- AI involvement: AI may flag language that suggests dependencies.
- Human review: Confirm external dependencies and lead times.
- Time target: 5 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if a prerequisite cannot be scheduled or obtained.

## 6. Prioritize work

- Purpose: Produce prioritized task list reliably.
- Input: Deadlines, effort ranges, dependencies, and course priorities
- Action: Rank tasks by deadline risk, importance, dependency impact, and effort.
- Tool: Priority worksheet
- Output: Prioritized task list
- AI involvement: AI may calculate a transparent first-pass order.
- Human review: The student makes the final priority decisions.
- Time target: 8 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if two priorities conflict and seek course guidance when needed.

## 7. Create a realistic weekly schedule

- Purpose: Produce weekly schedule reliably.
- Input: Prioritized task list and fixed commitments
- Action: Place focused blocks with buffers, breaks, and no more than the available weekly capacity.
- Tool: Calendar
- Output: Weekly schedule
- AI involvement: AI may propose a draft schedule without accessing private calendars.
- Human review: The student checks energy, accessibility needs, and feasibility.
- Time target: 12 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if planned work exceeds available capacity.

## 8. Create daily starting actions

- Purpose: Produce daily kickoff list reliably.
- Input: Weekly schedule
- Action: Write one small, concrete starting action for each scheduled work block.
- Tool: Notes or Calendar
- Output: Daily kickoff list
- AI involvement: AI may rewrite vague tasks into concrete starting actions.
- Human review: Confirm each action matches the actual assignment.
- Time target: 5 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if an action cannot be started with available materials.

## 9. Review unfinished work

- Purpose: Produce recovery decisions reliably.
- Input: Completed week and task status
- Action: Use the missed-task recovery process to reschedule, reduce, renegotiate, or remove unfinished work.
- Tool: Notes
- Output: Recovery decisions
- AI involvement: AI may present recovery options; it cannot make academic or health decisions.
- Human review: The student chooses a realistic recovery action.
- Time target: 7 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Escalate to an instructor or support service when a deadline cannot be met.

## 10. Update the following week

- Purpose: Produce updated planning baseline reliably.
- Input: Recovery decisions and observed timing
- Action: Carry forward only active work and update future effort estimates with actuals.
- Tool: Weekly intake and Calendar
- Output: Updated planning baseline
- AI involvement: AI may summarize timing differences from non-sensitive records.
- Human review: Confirm that completed or obsolete work is not carried forward.
- Time target: 5 minutes
- Stop or escalate if: Stop if the source of a carried-forward task is unclear.
